Overall Knowledge and Skills

All teachers require a large variety of knowledge and skills to effectively teach their students. Some knowledge and skills will be the same across all teachers; however, to most effectively teach their students their specific content areas the teacher may need additional skills and knowledge. Stage 4 technology mandatory is no exception to this phenomenon.

Work Health and Safety

“Schools have a legal obligation in relation to safety. Teachers need to ensure that they comply with relevant legislation as well as system and school requirements in relation to safety and risk management when implementing their programs.” (NESA, 2017, p21).

Ensuring and maintaining adequate workplace health and safety is critical in all aspects of life, professions and environments. The environment that technology classes may be taught in are quite varied and unique. Controlling the class in these environments to ensure the safety of everyone is key.

Technology classrooms are often a noisy environment that a mandatory technology teacher must compete with. For example; conversations and the noise of collaboration and machinery such as disk sanders and sewing machines. As a teacher, they need the skills and knowledge to gain the attention of the students when necessary for both safety and educational purposes.

Preparing Students for the Real World

We live in a constantly changing world, with technology being one of the fastest-changing areas. Teachers need to be both aware of these changes and have the skills and knowledge to match these changes to best teach and prepare students. This may including retraining to increase their skills and joining pages that inform teachers. Ultimately the goal is to prepare students for life after school.

Implementation of Practical Experiences

All sections of the stage 4 technology syllabus includes projects and practical skills, often in the form of project-based learning. Due to this time management skills are extremely crucial for a technology teacher. With this skills, they can demonstrate time management habits to the students, pack up class with plenty of time, and manage time so the students do not have to stop mid-task.

“To satisfy the requirements of the syllabus, students must undertake a range of practical experiences that occupy the majority of course time.” (NESA, 2017, p21)
